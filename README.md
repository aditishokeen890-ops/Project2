### Problem Statement 
Insurance companies often struggle to track and analyze large volumes of policy and claims data. Key challenges include:

Identifying which policy types generate the most revenue
Understanding claim patterns and high-risk customer segments
Monitoring policy activity (active vs inactive)
Improving claim settlement efficiency

This project aims to solve these challenges by building an interactive dashboard that provides clear insights into premiums, claims, customer demographics, and policy performance.

### Project Overview 
This project focuses on analyzing insurance data to uncover trends in premiums, claims, policy types, and customer demographics. The dashboard provides actionable insights for business decision-making.

### Business Insights:
#### Revenue Performance (Premium vs Coverage)
The company has generated 5.98M in premiums against a coverage exposure of 600.55M, indicating a large liability base. This suggests the need for careful risk management and pricing optimization.

#### Claims Risk Analysis
With total claims at 16.91M, and a significant portion being rejected, there may be:
1.Strict claim approval policies
2.Potential customer dissatisfaction
3.Opportunity to improve claim transparency

#### Policy Type Contribution
Travel Insurance dominates premium contribution (~2.5M), making it the most profitable segment. However, dependence on one segment may increase business risk.

#### Customer Activity Insights
Around 58% policies are active, which is a positive retention signal. However, 42% inactive policies highlight scope for:
1.Customer re-engagement strategies
2.Renewal campaigns

#### Demographic Insights (Age Group)
The adult segment generates the highest claim amount, indicating:
1.Higher policy usage
2.Potentially higher risk exposure in this group

#### Operational Efficiency (Claims Status)
Higher rejected claims vs settled claims may indicate inefficiencies in:
1.Claim verification process
2.Policy clarity
3.Customer communication
